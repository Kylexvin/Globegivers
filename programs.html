Our Programs & Services

Making a sustainable impact on Mfangano Island and surrounding communities

How We're Making a Difference

At GlobeGivers, we implement sustainable community-led programs that address the most pressing challenges facing Mfangano Island. Our holistic approach focuses on clean water access, sustainable agriculture, healthcare, empowerment, and infrastructure development.

The Challenge

Most families on Mfangano Island lack running water and rely on Lake Victoria for drinking, washing, and farming. However, the lake is heavily polluted with sewage, chemicals, and waste from three surrounding countries, posing significant health risks to the community.

Our Solution

To address this critical issue, we have established a clean water collection point serving up to 5,000 people daily. With a population of 37,000, we aim to expand access, reducing long travel distances for water.

Current Initiative: "Wakinga Community Clean Water Project"

Our ongoing project seeks to bring safe drinking water closer to more residents by:

  • Installing additional water collection points in underserved areas
  • Implementing water filtration systems
  • Training community members in water management and conservation
  • Establishing community-led water committees to ensure sustainability

The Challenge

Traditional farming methods on Mfangano Island have led to soil degradation, reduced biodiversity, and unsustainable land use. Many farmers struggle with decreasing yields and food insecurity as environmental conditions worsen.

Our Approach

We promote sustainable farming that balances food production, nature preservation, and rural livelihoods. By working with local farmers, we encourage:

  • Soil-nurturing crop rotation and companion planting
  • Biodiversity-friendly farming practices
  • Land-clearing methods that preserve trees and wildlife habitats
  • Water conservation techniques
  • Natural pest management solutions

  • School garden programs to teach children sustainable agriculture

The Challenge

Women and youth on Mfangano Island face limited economic opportunities, barriers to education, and restricted access to healthcare and financial resources. This perpetuates cycles of poverty and inequality.

Our Solution

We support women and youth in achieving financial independence through small businesses, education, and healthcare access. Most women are now part of registered groups offering financial support and skills development.

Women's & Youth Microfinance Initiative

Our microfinance program provides small loans that help start or expand businesses like:

  • Agricultural enterprises
  • Retail shops and market stalls
  • Beauty salons and tailoring services
  • Small hotels and food services
  • Transportation services

The Challenge

Access to healthcare on Mfangano Island is severely limited. Residents often travel long distances to reach medical facilities, which is particularly dangerous for emergency situations and maternal care. Many preventable conditions go untreated due to lack of affordable healthcare options.

Our Response

We are improving healthcare access by providing preventive services, first aid kits, and affordable medical care. Our Wakinga Community Health Center is expanding to offer low-cost and mostly free treatments, reducing health risks, especially for mothers giving birth far from clinics.

Wakinga Community Health Center

Currently undergoing phase two construction, our health center provides:

  • Basic emergency medical services
  • Maternal and child healthcare
  • Preventive care and vaccinations
  • Health education and outreach
  • First aid training for community members

The Challenge

Educational infrastructure on Mfangano and sister islands is severely underdeveloped. Many schools lack adequate facilities, qualified teachers, and learning materials. This results in low graduation rates and limited opportunities for higher education.

Our Approach

We improve local institutions, focusing on education. Our goal is to ensure students graduate high school, pursue higher education, and gain valuable skills. We support primary, high schools, and post-secondary education, with special focus on girls, orphans, and vulnerable children on Mfangano and sister islands.

Key Focus Areas

  • School facility improvement and construction
  • Educational materials and resources
  • Teacher training and support
  • Scholarship programs for vulnerable students
  • Community learning centers for adult education
